HTML+CSS & Marks+Channels

The marks are the several points on the graphs. The channels are the horizontal and vertical positions on the graph, as well as the size of the marks. Each area and color of each pie is also a channel for each pie chart. The values of the axis are quantitative data types. For the pie chart, the area of the pie is quantitative, while the color of the pie is catergorical. All the marks in this visualsation are items.

The marks are the individual bumps on the chart and the lines connecting each bump. The channels are the direction of each connections between each bump, as well as the color of each path. The y-axis which indicates rank is ordinal, and the years on the x-axis are categorical. The individual bumps are nodes, but the links between each bump are links.

The marks for this visualsation are the nodes on the graph, as well as the lines connecting the nodes. The channels are the direction of the connecting lines and the color of each node. The data type shown by the chart is categorical. Each node mark is used as a node and the lines are used as links.

Each bar in the chart, as well as the subsections of the bar are marks. The channels are the vertical heights of each bar and sub-bar as well as the color of each. The y-axis uses ordinal data, while the x-axis uses categorical. Each mark in this chart is an item.
